Evaluated whether a well-established library could replace the custom task scheduler (`libs/scheduler`), since the core need -- "cron, but it catches up on tasks missed while the machine was off" -- sounds like something off-the-shelf should cover.

Conclusion: keep the custom scheduler. No established library cleanly satisfies all of our requirements at once (cron schedules, a human/agent-editable config file as the source of truth, a long-lived Python service under supervisord, and replay of runs missed during downtime from on-disk state). The closest contender, APScheduler 3.x, can replay missed runs only with non-default settings and would replace just our small timing core while adding a config-reconciliation layer -- a net increase in complexity, not a reduction. OS-native options (systemd timers, anacron) fit the "catch up after downtime" need but don't fit the workspace's supervisord-based, non-systemd service model. No behavior changed.
